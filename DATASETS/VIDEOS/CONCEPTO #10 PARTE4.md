How we built a premium brand in eight weeks, including the strategy, logos, and visual identity. This is Titan Arc, a high-end health and longevity platform for ultra-high-net worth individuals. The challenge was to create multiple sub-brands under one unified system, from apps to summits. The whole project took eight weeks. Week one is Discovery where we ask questions like, what are your services? Who is it for? Why should they care? And who are you competing with? You then move these brand personality sliders to tell us how you want your brand to be perceived. Week three is where we present the brand strategy, like a company profile, brand personality, positioning, mission, vision, purpose, story, and ideal buyer personas. So Titan Arc was a health and longevity platform that was targeting ultra-high-net worth executives who refused average health care. So the brand story became your health on your terms. The brand personality, a combination of sage and magician, like Leonardo da Vinci, intelligent, insightful, transformative. Next comes the mood boards for look and feel. Moody board one feels like golden hour, warm tones, soft shadows, and close human moments. Moody board two feels like intelligence and motion, cool tones, data visuals, and structured layouts. Moody board three feels like the future of wellness, light neutrals with soft, uplifting gradients, clean, calm, and optimistic. The client preferred a blend of mood board one and mood board three, leaning 7030. By week five, we were showing three logo design concepts. Logo one was a prism concept derived from bringing intelligence, community, and human guidance together. This logo paired with cooler color tones and soft, both of visuals felt precise, intelligent, and slightly futuristic. Logo two was a symbol built from precise dots inspired by an eye, focused on clarity, early detection, and data, more structured, analytical, and systems driven. Logo three involved overlapping forms to represent connection and balance, more human, more expressive, focused on health as continuous living system. The client chose the prism concept. By week seven, we moved into design refinement. We brought the brand to life across various touch points. Each sub-brand got its own color, while the core system kept everything unified. The client felt the initial color palette felt too feminine, so we shifted to a golden hour tones, warmer, stronger, more grounded. Then we created symbols for each sub-brand, Titan Angel, a halo for guidance, Titan Prophet, stars for foresight, Titan Volt, structured for security, Titan Oracle, I for intelligence. Titan Summit, convergence of people coming together. By week eight, we are delivering the final branded empty guide that shows how the system works across website, digital and social media, stationary, and marketing collaterals. Summit banners, lanyards, screens. Then we locked it all in. Logos, identity, colors, typography, photography. A complete brand built in eight weeks, from strategy to mood boards, to design concepts, to refinement, to final execution. This is how premium brands are built.
