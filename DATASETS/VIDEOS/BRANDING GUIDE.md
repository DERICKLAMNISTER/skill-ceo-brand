Here's what goes into a brand identity guide. This is the brand refresh we did for Verrata, a Canadian company that builds assessment systems for educational institutions. A brand identity guide is your brand's rule book. It ensures everything from your website to your business card looks like it came from the same brain. Here are 20 things you can expect to see in a brand identity guide. First is the logo design. This is your core mark, the face of your brand. Second is the logo usage. Rules around how the logo should be used, either as a lockup or as a separate individual element. Next is logo construction, the invisible math that keeps it balanced, that makes sure that there's enough clear space around it so it's not overcrowded. Logo color variations, different versions for dark, light, as well as premium finishes like metallic copper foil stamping. Logo don'ts, what not to do with your logo. Color palette, a curated set of colors that represent your brand. Typography, these are the fonts you use to represent your brand. Photography style, what your brand feels like visually, both for portraits, as well as for lifestyle photography with lighting, mood, and tone. Next we go into the website design system where we show a couple of slides of how your website homepage could look like. Stationery and collaterals, everything from your business cards, letter heads, e-signature, report covers, to billboards and land years. Next comes presentation decks, how your PowerPoint slides show up in meetings, e-newsletters, how your brand communicates through email which your customers or clients. Signage, how your brand shows up in interior design space. Gifts and giveaways, these are details that make your brand memorable beyond the screens. Finally, an animated logo, using motion graphics to add flair and personality to your brand. Remember, a brand is not just a logo, but an entire system that works together consistently to build trust. And trust is what makes a brand premium. Follow for more premium branding advice.
