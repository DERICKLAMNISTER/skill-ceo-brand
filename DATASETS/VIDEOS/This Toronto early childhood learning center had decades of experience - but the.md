How we turned an early education center from basic to brilliant. This is the brand refresh we did for CFEL, Centers for Early Learning, a network of early learning education centers in Toronto dedicated to helping children grow through curiosity, play and community. The whole project took eight weeks, starting with Discovery, where we ask questions like what are your services? Who is it for? Why should they care? And who are you competing with? You then move these brand personality sliders to tell us how you want your brand to be perceived. By week three, we're presenting the brand strategy, like your company profile, brand personality, positioning, mission, vision, story, and ideal buyer persona. So this center provides early education programs for children from infancy to school age. The audience are professional parents seeking quality care and education for their kids. Their story is all about seeing big potential in little learners. Their brand personality is that of the mentor. Think, build an eye the science guy, knowledgeable, nurturing and trustworthy. Not that we have the branding framework complete, next comes mood boards for look and feel. Mooboad one structured play, a clean minimal direction inspired by building blocks, puzzles and geometric forms. Mooboad two, heartfelt scribbles, a warm, playful direction with organic shapes, expressive illustrations, and friendly characters. Mooboad three, nurturing narratives, a more artistic and sophisticated direction, combining storytelling with natural textures and calming colors. The client chose Mooboad three. By week five, we're showing three logo design concepts. Logo option one creates a smiley face with the initials CFEL. Simple, rounded forms that feel playful yet structured, reflecting both learning and growth. Logo option two was another rendition of the smiley face using the initials. This time using a sans-serif font and squiggly line illustrations, inspired by children's creativity and imagination. Mooboad three shows a symbol of a smiley face contained in a sea with geometric illustrations using modular shapes and gradients to express curiosity and creativity. They like the logo of option one, but they also like some of the squiggly line illustrations from the visual identity of option two. By week seven, we're into design refinement, and by week eight we deliver the complete brand identity guide fully packaged in Adobe Illustrator. This includes mockups of business cards and corporate stationery like letterheads and folders. A website homepage design that guides visitors through the different programs they offer, an example of how the brand lives across social media and digital ads. We also show how the brand comes to life inside the facility, from wayfinding signage to large scale walled mural illustrations that reflect the brand's personality. Then we lock the system, logo variations, color palettes, fonts, and custom illustrations. A complete brand identity in eight weeks, from strategy to mood boards, to design explorations, and final execution. This is how brands are built. Follow for more premium branding advice.
