How to create a great brand name. The biggest brands have the shortest names. Nike, Lego, Apple, Google, PayPal, all two syllable words. If people can't say your name in one breath, they probably won't remember it. Abbreviations don't help either. That's why Federal Express became FedEx. Duncan Donuts became Duncan. And the Walt Disney Company just Disney. At Raven, we take naming seriously. During our brand strategy process is when we present name options. We create three mood boards, each representing a distinct look, feel, and personality. Then we craft three name options that align perfectly with each mood board's vibe. That's how we landed on Champa for this Gen Z nut snack brand. It's short, bold, playful, and literally sounds like a bite. Champ, Champ. When naming your brand, keep it short, two to three syllables max, easy to pronounce, memorable, and related to your category. Follow for more premium branding advice.
