How do you rebrand a 20 year old company? Here's how we rebranded Pet Products Trading Company into Montajat, one of the largest pioneer leaders of the Pet Industry in Saudi Arabia and the GCC. We will explore the secret meaning behind this new symbol and how it all launched with great fanfare for their 20th anniversary. But first, what does a small branding agency in Canada know about the Pet Industry in Saudi Arabia? Absolutely nothing. That's why we kicked off with Discovery, asking questions like, what do you do? Who it's for? Why should they care? And who are you competing with? We then move these brand personality sliders to figure out how you want your brand to be perceived. Next comes the strategy, where we map out your product, your audience, your story, and your brand personality. Based on this, we put together three mood boards for the look and feel. Option 1. Warm, expressive, bold. Option 2. Fresh, professional, clean. Option 3. Minimal, trustworthy, contemporary. The client chose the first mood board. Next came the logo designs. The first featured a line illustration of a cat and a dog with bright friendly colors. The second, used bold font and acute cat mascot with heart shaped paws. The visual identity uses shapes inspired by Pet Products, as well as warm yellow and blue color palette. The third was a type based logo using negative space in the M to suggest a pet, with earthy tones and hand drawn illustrations for a grounded friendly look. The client chose the second option, but wanted to avoid highlighting a specific animal, so we created a heart shaped symbol that cleverly combines the English letter M for Montajat, which is Arabic for products, the letter P for products, which also serves as the meme, the Arabic letter M. The logo was brought to life with simple icons, blue icons on the left for B to B logistics, and yellow icons on the right for pet care. The brand identity extended across everything, from business cards and corporate stationery, website, social media, store signage, van decals and uniforms. We even revamped the locally made cat food line, giving it the bold, shelf ready look that feels vibrant and proudly rooted in Saudi. They unveiled their new brand on their 20th anniversary this year, with lights, drums, big stage and full fanfare. This is what premium branding should feel like, from forgettable to formidable.
