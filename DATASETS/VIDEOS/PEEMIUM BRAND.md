How to create a premium brand in 8 weeks, from strategy to naming to logo and visual identity. Meet Bureau, a high-end architecture and construction company that designs, builds, and fully furnishes modular homes delivered in 3-6 months, not years. The whole project took 8 weeks. Week 1 is Discovery where we ask questions like, what are your services? Who is it for? What should they care? And who are you competing with? You then move these brand personality sliders to tell us how you want your brand to be perceived. Week 3 is where we present the brand strategy, like a company profile, brand personality, positioning, mission, vision, purpose, story, and ideal buyer personas. So these guys construct prefabricated modular homes that they want to sell to visionary homeowners. Their story, design, build, furnish, faster. Their personality, a combination of creator and magician archetype, thinks Sir John Ive, innovative, visionary, empowering. Now that we have the framework complete, next comes the mood boards for look and feel and name options. Move board 1, this direction had a Scandinavian inspired aesthetic paired with architectural names like Stella, Arbol, Braun. Move board 2, this direction had a confident precision-led aesthetic, inspired by modular engineering and clean geometry. Names we came up with were Strux, Trillo, and Viro for Strength. Move board 3, this was a one modern aesthetic that balances architectural clarity with nature. Names we came up with were Vivaro, which means to live, Skulf, which means Forest, and Kalo, which means Cave. The client chose mood board 1 and the name Viro. By week 5 we were showing three logo design concepts. Logo option 1, a modern word mark paired with a modular visual language that expresses engineering, movement, and precision. In a design forward way, reinforcing Viro's promise of building beautifully faster. Logo option 2, start with type, strong, architectural, confident. We built a visual system by bringing beveled rectangular forms together, inspired by construction blocks, floor plates, and how homes are actually assembled. Logo option 3, a system of stackable geometric blocks, equing modular construction. Each shape is a module, each layout is configurable. The client liked logo and visual identity option 1, and the color palette of option 2. By week 7 we're into design refinement, and week 8 we deliver the complete brand identity guide, fully packaged in Adobe Illustrator. This includes business cards, letterheads, portfolios, and website user experience from messaging, voice and tone to visual style, graphics, and photography. We show how the brand lives across social content, digital ads, investor presentation decks, and poster campaigns. Logo and visual identity, color palette, and typography. A full brand identity in 8 weeks, from strategy to naming, to design, and final execution. This is how brands are built.
